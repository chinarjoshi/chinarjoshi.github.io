To those who think Linux users are eccentric and their user space is archaic, I couldn’t agree more. Non-profit work from disparate programmers will never match the polish of Apple products powered by the industry’s best engineers and their ultra-optimized neural networks. However, the fact that Linux servers power the state of the art of AI, yet Linux users don’t see a lick of it, is possibly the worst trade deal of the Unix epoch.

I want to change this incrementally by documenting specific problems I face using the Linux desktop in James Mickens-style and solving them through simple, open-source programs and machine learning solutions. In doing so, I want to bridge the gap between Linux enthusiasts and AI enthusiasts, two significant yet disparate communities. If you find a project that piques your interest, please consider getting involved and contributing!
