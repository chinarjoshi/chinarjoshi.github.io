To those who think Linux users are eccentric and their user space is archaic, I couldn’t agree more. Non-profit work from disparate programmers cannot match the polish of Apple products powered by the industry’s best engineers and their ultra-optimized neural networks. However, the fact that Linux servers power the state of the art of AI, yet Linux users don’t see a lick of it, is possibly the worst trade deal of the Unix epoch.

I aim to incrementally enhance the Linux desktop by first articulating specific usability problems I encounter in James Mickens-style, and second designing simple open-source solutions in the form of programs and machine learning applications. In doing so, I want to bridge the gap between Linux enthusiasts and AI enthusiasts, two significant yet disparate communities. If you find a project that piques your interest, please consider getting involved and contributing!
